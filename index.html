About

My research examines how digitization changes consumer behavior and firms' product distribution strategies, combining field experiments run in industry settings with big data analytics and machine learning.

My current work centers on two areas:

  • Artificial intelligence — how generative AI is reshaping the skills markets reward, the institutions that certify them, and the way firms and workers adapt.
  • Data availability and data protection — how access to data, and regulation such as the GDPR, shape consumer behavior and firm outcomes.

This agenda grows out of two earlier threads that continue to inform my work, though I pursue them less actively today:

  • Digitization of the creative industries — how the shift to digital distribution reshaped consumption, piracy, and firm strategy.
  • Social networks and peer influence — how connections between consumers shape adoption, churn, and word of mouth.
