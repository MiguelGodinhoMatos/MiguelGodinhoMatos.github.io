About

My research examines how digitization changes consumer behavior and firms' product distribution strategies. Combining field experiments run in industry settings with big data analytics, my agenda spans three connected areas:

  • Digitization and the creative industries — how the shift to digital distribution reshapes consumption, piracy, and firm strategy.
  • Social networks and peer influence — how connections between consumers shape adoption, churn, and word of mouth.
  • Data availability and data-protection law — how access to data, and regulation such as the GDPR, affect consumer behavior and firm outcomes.

Hiring

I am recruiting post-doctoral fellows to work on large-data analytics in the economics of digitization. Two profiles are of particular interest:

Position 1 — Economics / Econometrics

  • PhD in economics, econometrics, statistics, or a related field
  • Background in microeconomics, game theory, and consumer-behavior theory
  • Experience with structural modeling and empirical research
  • Interest in applied Information Systems work

Position 2 — Computer Science / ML

  • PhD in computer science, statistics, or a related field
  • Expertise in machine learning and data mining (SVMs, gradient boosting, decision trees, etc.)

Both positions: proficiency in SQL and R; knowledge of Python (NumPy, SciPy, scikit-learn) is highly valued.

To apply, send a copy of your vita and a brief statement of research interests to miguel.godinhomatos@clsbe.lisboa.ucp.pt.
